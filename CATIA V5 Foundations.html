Overview

What is CATIA?

CATIA stands for Computer Aided Three-dimensional Interactive Application. CATIA is a powerful 3D software and it is used in a wide range of industries, including aerospace, automotive, industrial design, and architecture, among others. The software allows designers and engineers to create and manipulate 3D models of products, parts, and assemblies with a high degree of precision and accuracy.

CATIA is not just a 3D modeling software, but a complete product development solution that includes tools for design, simulation, analysis, manufacturing, and collaboration. It is used by designers, engineers, and manufacturers to create products from concept to production, and to streamline the product development process.

What is CATIA Designer?

CATIA Designer is a module within the CATIA suite that is specifically designed for product design and engineering.

CATIA Designer allows designers to create, modify, and analyze 3D models of products and parts. It has a comprehensive set of tools for sketching, modeling, surfacing, and assembly design. It also offers a wide range of simulation and anal- ysis tools to test the performance of the product under different conditions.

With CATIA Designer, designers can create complex shapes and geometries with precision and accuracy. It provides a collaborative environment for team members to work together on the same project, enabling efficient communication and design iterations. Additionally, CATIA Designer is often used in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and industrial equipment manufacturing.

What can be learned?

1. 3D modeling: CATIA Designer provides a comprehensive set of tools for creat- ing and modifying 3D models of automotive parts and assemblies. You can learn how to create complex shapes, surfaces, and geometries with precision and accuracy.

2. Assembly design: In CATIA Designer, you can learn how to design and assem- ble automotive parts and subassemblies. You can also learn how to manage com- plex assembly structures and constraints.

3. Kinematics analysis: CATIA Designer offers a range of simulation and analysis tools to test the performance of automotive parts and assemblies. You can learn how to analyze the kinematics of suspension systems, steering mechanisms, and other automotive components.

4. Sheet metal design: With CATIA Designer, you can learn how to design and manufacture sheet metal parts for automotive applications. You can also learn how to create flat patterns and generate manufacturing drawings.

5. Electrical and electronics design: CATIA Designer also includes tools for designing electrical and electronics components for automotive applications. You can learn how to design wiring harnesses, connectors, and other electrical components.

Overall, CATIA Designer provides a powerful set of tools for automotive design and engineering. By learning how to use CATIA Designer, you can develop the skills needed to design and manufacture high-quality automotive parts and assemblies.

Curriculum

INTRODUCTION

Learn CATIA V5 basics: parametric modeling, UI navigation, file management, shape creation/ editing using profiles, planes, axes, and tools.

SKETCHER

Become proficient in CATIA V5 sketching: planes, axes, corner chamfers, constraints, 3D projection, and transformations across various toolsets.

PART DESIGN

Master CATIA V5 part Modeling: body, shaft, patterns, fillets, advanced features, materials, rendering, and efficient manipulation techniques like copy-paste and parameters.

ASSEMBLY DESIGN

Excel in CATIA V5 assembly: components, constraints, sub-assemblies, clash prevention, tree management, replacements, numbering, multi-instantiation for efficient work.

DETAILING

Gain CATIA V5 drafting skills: view creation, annotation, dimensioning, tolerancing, toolbar usage, frame/title block insertion for precise engineering drawings.
